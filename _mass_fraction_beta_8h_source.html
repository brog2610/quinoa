00002 /*!
00003   \file      src/DiffEq/MassFractionBeta.h
00004   \author    J. Bakosi
00005   \date      Mon 01 Jun 2015 02:37:52 PM MDT
00006   \copyright 2012-2015, Jozsef Bakosi.
00007   \brief     System of mass-fraction beta SDEs
00008   \details   This file implements the time integration of a system of stochastic
00009     differential equations (SDEs) with linear drift and quadratic diagonal
00010     diffusion, whose invariant is the joint [beta
00011     distribution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beta_distribution). The main
00012     difference compared to the plain beta SDE (see DiffEq/Beta.h), is that in
00013     the mass-fraction beta SDE the dependent variable, there are two
00014     additional stochastic variables computed from the beta variables.
00015 
00016     In a nutshell, the equation integrated governs a set of scalars,
00017     \f$0\!\le\!Y_\alpha\f$, \f$\alpha\!=\!1,\dots,N\f$, as
00018     \f[
00019        \mathrm{d}Y_\alpha(t) = \frac{b_\alpha}{2}\left(S_\alpha - Y_\alpha\right)
00020        \mathrm{d}t + \sqrt{\kappa_\alpha Y_\alpha(1-Y_\alpha)}
00021        \mathrm{d}W_\alpha(t), \qquad \alpha=1,\dots,N
00022     \f]
00023     with parameter vectors \f$b_\alpha > 0\f$, \f$\kappa_\alpha > 0\f$, and \f$0
00024     < S_\alpha < 1\f$. This is the same as in DiffEq/Beta.h. Here
00025     \f$\mathrm{d}W_\alpha(t)\f$ is an isotropic vector-valued [Wiener
00026     process](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiener_process) with independent
00027     increments. The invariant distribution is the joint beta distribution. This
00028     system of SDEs consists of N independent equations. For
00029     more on the beta SDE, see http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/14685248.2010.510843.
00030 
00031     In addition to integrating the above SDE, there are two additional functions
00032     of \f$ Y_\alpha \f$ are computed as
00033     \f[ \begin{align}
00034       \rho(Y_\alpha) & = \frac{ \rho_{2\alpha} }{ 1 + r_\alpha Y_\alpha } \\
00035       V(Y_\alpha) & = \frac{1}{ \rho(Y_\alpha) }
00036     \end{align} \f]
00037     These equations compute the instantaneous mixture density, \f$ \rho \f$, and
00038     instantaneous specific volume, \f$ V_\alpha \f$, for equation \f$ \alpha \f$
00039     in the system. These quantities are used in binary mixing of
00040     variable-density turbulence between two fluids with constant densities, \f$
00041     \rho_1, \f$ and \f$ \rho_2 \f$. The additional parameters, \f$ \rho_2 \f$
00042     and \f$ r' \f$ are user input parameters and kept constant during
00043     integration. Since we compute the above variables, \f$\rho,\f$ and \f$V\f$,
00044     and call them mixture density and specific volume, respectively, \f$Y\f$,
00045     governed by the beta SDE is a mass fraction, hence the name mass-fraction
00046     beta.
00047 
00048     _All of this is unpublished, but will be linked in here once published_.
00049 */
