00002 /*!
00003   \file      src/DiffEq/MixMassFractionBeta.h
00004   \author    J. Bakosi

00006   \copyright 2012-2015, Jozsef Bakosi.
00007   \brief     System of mix mass-fraction beta SDEs
00008   \details   This file implements the time integration of a system of stochastic
00009     differential equations (SDEs) with linear drift and quadratic diagonal
00010     diffusion, whose invariant is the joint [beta
00011     distribution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beta_distribution). There are two
00012     differences compared to the plain beta SDE (see DiffEq/Beta.h):
00013 
00014     - First, the parameters, b, and kappa are specified via functions that
00015     constrain the beta SDE to be consistent with the turbulent mixing process.
00016     In particular, the SDE is made consistent with the no-mix and fully mixed
00017     limits. See, e.g., MixMassFractionBetaCoeffConst::update().
00018 
00019     - Second, there two additional random variables computed, the same as also
00020     computed by the mass-fraction beta equation, see also
00021     DiffEq/MassFractionBeta.h.
00022 
00023     In a nutshell, the equation integrated governs a set of scalars,
00024     \f$0\!\le\!Y_\alpha\f$, \f$\alpha\!=\!1,\dots,N\f$, as
00025     \f[
00026        \mathrm{d}Y_\alpha(t) = \frac{b_\alpha}{2}\left(S_\alpha - Y_\alpha\right)
00027        \mathrm{d}t + \sqrt{\kappa_\alpha Y_\alpha(1-Y_\alpha)}
00028        \mathrm{d}W_\alpha(t), \qquad \alpha=1,\dots,N
00029     \f]
00030     with parameter vectors \f$b_\alpha = \Theta b'_\alpha > 0\f$, \f$
00031     \newcommand{\irv}[1]{\langle{#1^2}\rangle} \kappa_\alpha = \kappa' \irv{x} >
00032     0\f$, and \f$0 < S_\alpha < 1\f$. This is similar to DiffEq/Beta.h, but the
00033     parameters, \f$b\f$ and \f$\kappa\f$ constrained. Here \f$
00034     \newcommand{\irv}[1]{\langle{#1^2}\rangle}
00035     \newcommand{\irmean}[1]{{\langle{#1}\rangle}} \Theta = 1 - \irv{x} /
00036     [ \irmean{Y} (1-\irmean{Y}) ]\f$. The fluctuation about the mean, \f$
00037     \newcommand{\irmean}[1]{{\langle{#1}\rangle}} \irmean{Y} \f$, is defined as
00038     usual: \f$ \newcommand{\irmean}[1]{{\langle{#1}\rangle}} x = Y - \irmean{Y}
00039     \f$, and \f$b'\f$ and \f$ \kappa'\f$ are user-specified constants. Also,
00040     \f$\mathrm{d}W_\alpha(t)\f$ is an isotropic vector-valued
00041     [Wiener process](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiener_process) with
00042     independent increments. The invariant distribution is the joint beta
00043     distribution. This system of SDEs consists of N independent equations. For
00044     more on the beta SDE, see http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/14685248.2010.510843.
00045 
00046     In addition to integrating the above SDE, there are two additional functions
00047     of \f$ Y_\alpha \f$ are computed as
00048     \f[ \begin{align}
00049       \rho(Y_\alpha) & = \frac{ \rho_{2\alpha} }{ 1 + r_\alpha Y_\alpha } \\
00050       V(Y_\alpha) & = \frac{1}{ \rho(Y_\alpha) }
00051     \end{align} \f]
00052     These equations compute the instantaneous mixture density, \f$ \rho \f$, and
00053     instantaneous specific volume, \f$ V_\alpha \f$, for equation \f$ \alpha \f$
00054     in the system. These quantities are used in binary mixing of
00055     variable-density turbulence between two fluids with constant densities, \f$
00056     \rho_1, \f$ and \f$ \rho_2 \f$. The additional parameters, \f$ \rho_2 \f$
00057     and \f$ r \f$ are user input parameters and kept constant during
00058     integration. Since we compute the above variables, \f$\rho,\f$ and \f$V\f$,
00059     and call them mixture density and specific volume, respectively, \f$Y\f$,
00060     governed by the beta SDE is a mass fraction.
00061 
00062     _All of this is unpublished, but will be linked in here once published_.
00063 */
