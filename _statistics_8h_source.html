00007   \brief     Statistics class declaration
00008   \details   This file implements a statistics class that can be used to
00009     estimate statistics from an ensemble. Supported at this time are ordinary
00010     and central statistical moments of arbitrary-length products and arbitrary
00011     number of 1D, 2D, and 3D probability density functions (PDF) with sample
00012     spaces of ordinary and/or central sample space variables.
00013 
00014     _Definitions and nomenclature:_
00015 
00016     - Upper-case letters denote a full random variable, e.g., X
00017 
00018     - Lower-case letters denote a fluctuation about the mean, i.e., x = X - <X>
00019 
00020     - Letters can be augmented by a field ID, i.e., X2 is the full variable of
00021       the second component of the vector X, while x1 = X1 - <X1> is the
00022       fluctuation about the mean of the first component of vector X.
00023 
00024     - If the field ID is unspecified, it defaults to the first field, i.e.,
00025       X = X1, x = x1, etc.
00026 
00027     - Statistical moments of arbitrary-length products can be computed.
00028 
00029       Examples:
00030       - <X> - mean,
00031       - <xx> - variance,
00032       - <xxx> - third central moment,
00033       - <xy> - covariance of X and Y,
00034       - <x1y2> - covariance of the first component of vector X and the second
00035         component of vector Y
00036 
00037     - In general, arbitrary-length products can be estimated that make up a
00038       statistical moment, using any number and combinations of upper and
00039       lower-case letters and their field IDs < [A-Za-z][1-9] ... >.
00040 
00041     - A statistical moment is ordinary if and only if all of its terms are
00042       ordinary. A central moment has at least one term that is central, i.e., a
00043       fluctuation about its mean.
00044 
00045       - Examples of ordinary moments: <X>, <XX>, <XYZ>, etc.
00046       - Examples of central moments: <x1x2>, <Xy>, <XYz>, etc.
00047 
00048     - Estimation of the PDFs can be done using either ordinary or central sample
00049       space variables.
00050 
00051       Examples:
00052       - p(X) denotes the univariate PDF of the full variable X,
00053       - f(x1,x2) denotes the bivariate joint PDF of the fluctuations of the
00054         variables x1 and x2 about their respective means,
00055       - g(X,y,Z2) denotes the trivariate joint PDF of variables X, y = Y - <Y>,
00056         and Z2.
00057 */
